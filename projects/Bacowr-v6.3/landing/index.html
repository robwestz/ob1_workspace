Quality Guarantee

11 checks. Zero compromises.

Every article passes a binary QA gate before delivery. If any check fails, the article gets revised — not shipped.

✓ Word Count — 750–900 words, every time
✓ Anchor Present — Your exact link text, verified
✓ Anchor Count — Exactly 1 occurrence, no over-optimization
✓ Anchor Position — Placed at word 250–550, mid-article
✓ Trust Links — 1–2 verified third-party sources
✓ No Bullets/Lists — Flowing editorial prose only
✓ Heading Structure — Maximum 1 heading (title only)
✓ AI-Smell Detection — Zero forbidden AI phrases
✓ Language Match — Correct language for target domain
✓ SERP Entities — Min. 4 domain-specific entities woven in
✓ Paragraph Depth — Min. 4 substantive paragraphs

Frequently asked questions

ChatGPT and Jasper are general-purpose writers. They produce text based on a prompt, but they don't understand your link-building strategy. Bacowr is purpose-built for backlink content: it profiles the publisher, fingerprints your target page, calculates the semantic distance between them, runs 5 SERP research queries to understand what Google knows about your topic, discovers trust links, and then builds a structured blueprint before writing a single word. Every article passes an 11-check automated QA gate. The result is content that reads naturally in the publisher's context while strategically placing your anchor link.
Each article takes approximately 60–90 seconds to process through the full 8-phase pipeline, including SERP research. A batch of 100 articles typically completes in 2–3 hours. Pro and Agency tiers get priority/dedicated processing queues for faster throughput. You can track progress in real time on your dashboard.
Every article is generated from a unique blueprint built from the specific publisher-target pair. The semantic bridge calculation, SERP research results, and publisher profile are different for each job, so the content naturally varies. Even if two jobs share the same target URL, different publishers produce different articles because the bridge strategy adapts to each publisher's domain and topic context. Our QA gate also checks for forbidden AI patterns that tend to create templated-sounding content.
